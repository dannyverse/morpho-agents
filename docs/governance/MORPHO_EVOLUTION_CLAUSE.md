# EVOLUTION PRINCIPLE

When evidence contradicts a hypothesis:

The hypothesis should change.

Not reality.

Healthy systems are loyal to reality.

Not to their previous assumptions.

---

# WHEN SHOULD CAPITAL STOP?

Capital deployment should stop when evidence no longer justifies the current hypothesis.

Stopping capital does not imply stopping Morpho.

Stopping capital creates space for:

```text
Learning
↓

Reflection
↓

New Hypothesis
↓

New Experiment
```

Reality determines whether a hypothesis deserves continuation.

Not emotional attachment.

---

# WHEN SHOULD MORPHO DIE?

Not when a strategy fails.

Not when a hypothesis fails.

Not when a market changes.

Morpho should only cease evolving when:

* no meaningful learning emerges
* no new hypotheses justify experimentation
* reality repeatedly provides no evidence worth pursuing
* curiosity becomes disconnected from evidence
* architecture becomes a substitute for reality

The death of hypotheses is healthy.

The death of learning is not.
