---

# TRUTH VS KNOWLEDGE

## Truth

Represents:

```text
What is real.
```

Examples:

* positions
* executions
* protocol balances
* deployed exposure

Truth describes reality.

---

## Knowledge

Represents:

```text
What experience suggests.
```

Examples:

* opportunity quality
* edge persistence
* failed hypotheses
* successful hypotheses
* historical observations

Knowledge interprets experience.

---

# IMPORTANT OBSERVATION

Truth and Knowledge are not interchangeable.

Truth governs reality.

Knowledge informs judgment.

These domains should remain separate.

---

# PRINCIPLE 1

## Knowledge Must Not Contaminate Economic Reality

Knowledge should never reside inside canonical economic state.

Examples:

```text
position_state
executions
protocol snapshots
```

must remain:

```text
economic reality
```

and should not become repositories for:

* hypotheses
* predictions
* expected decay
* edge quality assumptions
* learning metadata

---

### Example

Bad:

```json
{
  "asset": "DOGE",
  "side": "SHORT",
  "entry_price": 0.18,
  "expected_decay": "48h",
  "hypothesis_id": 17
}
```

This mixes:

```text
Economic Reality
+
Knowledge
```

and violates ontological separation.

---

# PRINCIPLE 2

## Knowledge Is Advisory, Not Sovereign

Knowledge may influence decisions.

Knowledge may never become reality.

Allowed:

```text
Past opportunities suggest caution.
```

Not allowed:

```text
Past opportunities define reality.
```

Knowledge informs.

Truth governs.

---

# PRINCIPLE 3

## Observability Before Autonomous Learning

In accordance with:

```text
Principle 9
Observability Before Automation
```

Learning should become visible before becoming autonomous.

---

### Preferred Pattern

```text
Opportunity detected
↓
Historical warning generated
↓
Operator informed
↓
Decision remains observable
```

This preserves:

* auditability
* governability
* human understanding

before introducing automation.

---

# PRINCIPLE 4

## Learning Must Remain Explainable

Future learning systems should explain:

* what was learned
* when it was learned
* why it was learned
* what evidence supported the conclusion

A system that cannot explain its learning cannot be governed safely.

---

# RESEARCH TOPIC

## False Positive Learning

A single outcome should not automatically create a permanent conclusion.

Examples:

* temporary liquidity anomaly
* protocol outage
* market regime shift
* execution failure

Future learning systems may require:

* evidence weighting
* confidence levels
* contextual learning
* temporal relevance

---

# RESEARCH TOPIC

## Knowledge Decay

An important unanswered question:

```text
Should knowledge remain permanent?
```

Examples:

* an edge disappears
* an edge reappears
* market structure changes
* opportunity dynamics evolve

Future systems may require:

```text
knowledge decay
```

similar to:

```text
edge decay
```

This remains a research topic only.

---

# RESEARCH TOPIC

## Institutional Memory

A long-term objective may eventually become:

```text
Prevent repeating old mistakes.
```

Examples:

* failed opportunities
* governance failures
* reconciliation failures
* incorrect assumptions

The challenge becomes transforming:

```text
Experience
```

into:

```text
Reusable Knowledge
```

without sacrificing:

* simplicity
* observability
* governability

---

# RESEARCH TOPIC

## Knowledge Confidence

Future knowledge systems may eventually need to distinguish between:

```text
Observation
```

and:

```text
Confidence In Observation
```

Examples:

```text
Edge appears profitable
Confidence: High
```

versus:

```text
Edge appears profitable
Confidence: Low
```

This mirrors future questions already emerging inside Reconciliation Philosophy.

No implementation required.

Research only.
